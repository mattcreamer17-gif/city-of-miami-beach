01

The stack, as it reads from the outside

Miami Beach runs a mid-size city on a barrier island with a 24/7 resort economy on top of it. The FY2026 IT posture names a short list: the integrated customer-service dashboard, the building-department digital push, the cross-department data layer, surveillance analytics with MBPD, and cyber resilience. The city is also 15 percent into a ten-year, $600 million stormwater and sea-level-rise program that FEMA now points to as a national model. That program pulls a lot of documents. 02

The working view: 60, 30, 10

The view we work from: most problems inside a city stack are not AI problems. The job is to figure out which piece of the customer-service dashboard, the permitting workflow, or the grant package sits in which bucket, and to build only what belongs in the top 10. Everything else runs faster and cheaper in Postgres.

60%

Traditional code and database work

The backbone of a customer-service dashboard is plumbing. Schema design, ETL, ArcGIS layers, indexed lookups. This does not need a model in the loop.

30%

Rule-based logic

Permitting routing, code-enforcement triage, grant-compliance checklists. A well-written Python script and a good schema already handle this without a call to an API.

10%

Genuine AI work

Cross-system summarization. Grant narrative assembly. Resident-facing status phrasing. This is the layer where an LLM earns its seat.

03

Precedent: the shape of the climate-grant reporting problem

We did this work at a mining-compliance company called Armetour. The team was burning 160-plus hours on a single compliance cycle. We built a multi-agent document layer on top of the systems they already ran. The same cycle now closes in under five minutes, with the human review intact.

The shape of Miami Beach's climate-grant reporting is the same shape. Structured inputs, regulated outputs, recurring cycle, real consequences for getting it wrong. We would expect the time compression to be closer to 10x than 30x on day one, and to improve as the document set stabilizes.

04

Methodology a Commissioner can read

The methodology is public. Interpretable Context Methodology, submitted to ACM TiiS. Every output is readable by a Commissioner without a briefing. When the city has to explain why a system made the call it made, the explanation is already in the folder.

05

Where the partner layer sits

Eduba partners with NLP Logix for work that sits below the orchestration layer. NLP Logix has been in machine learning since 2011 and runs over 150 data scientists. If a customer-service dashboard needs a real warehouse underneath it, or if surveillance analytics with MBPD grows beyond vendor-supplied analytics, that work routes through NLP Logix under the existing partnership.

The case studies Eduba leans on for this kind of work: 1,500+ people trained at Pacific Life and Colgate-Palmolive since May 2025, 6,000 to 9,000 hours saved per year, 95 percent still using the tools 30 days after the workshop. KPMG UK, one of the Big Four, 40+ executives trained. The Mayor's innovation-hub framing and the city's eMerge presence sit inside that same regulated, leadership-audience lane.
